Analysis

These 6 bars span C0 to P3 — scores 3 to 64 — a 20× spread among products all marketed as “protein bars.”

Key Takeaways

  • Two bars (Larabar, RXBAR) are essentially clean — whole ingredients, minimal or no additives.
  • The jump from RXBAR (3) to Clif (28) is where industrial formulation begins: protein isolates, emulsifiers, sweetener stacking.
  • More protein doesn’t mean more processing. RXBAR delivers 12 g at a score of 3; David packs 28 g but scores 64.
  • David’s MLS = 0 despite being the most processed — non-nutritive sweeteners and sugar alcohols dodge every metabolic flag while stacking 4 Tier A additives.

Sub-score Deep Dive

  • Each bar’s dominant sub-score differs: MLS drives Larabar, MDS drives GoMacro, AFS dominates David, HES peaks at Kind.
  • Kind’s HES = 14 (highest) comes from sweetener stacking — honey + sugar + glucose syrup creates a confection pattern.
  • David’s AFS = 39 is extraordinary — EPG (fat replacer), sucralose, acesulfame K, plus natural flavors account for 4 Tier A hits alone.
  • GoMacro shows MDS without AFS: organic pea protein isolate and brown rice syrup disrupt the ingredient matrix but add zero chemical additives.
