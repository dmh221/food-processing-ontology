Analysis

Seven yogurts, scores 0 to 51. The one marketed as “Light” is the most processed. Plain Greek yogurt with 5 ingredients scores 0; Light + Fit — engineered to remove fat and sugar — needs sucralose, acesulfame K, artificial flavors, modified starch, and a preservative to taste like yogurt again.

Key Takeaways

  • Fage Total 5% (0) is the baseline — milk and cultures, nothing else. Whole-milk fat is present but the ingredient list is as short as yogurt gets.
  • Fage Total 0% Blueberry (16) is the same brand with a blueberry fruit preparation — natural flavor, locust bean gum, pectin, and lemon juice concentrate push it to P1b. NOVA jumps from Group 1 to Group 4 for adding a fruit prep.
  • Siggi’s (9) introduces the first formulation markers: natural flavors and fruit pectin. Still clean by most standards, but FIS detects the step.
  • Chobani’s “fruit on the bottom” (20) reveals that the “fruit” is a manufactured component — juice concentrates, two gums, and colors.
  • Yoplait Original (27) is mainstream processing: modified food starch, gelatin, corn starch, and carmine (a red colorant from cochineal insects).
  • Light + Fit Strawberry (40) is the paradox: 3 Tier A additives (sucralose, acesulfame K, artificial flavor) replace the sugar and fat that were removed. MLS = 0 because there’s nothing left to flag metabolically.
  • Light + Fit Banana Cream (51) is the extreme: same 3 Tier A hits plus xanthan gum, potassium sorbate, pectin, malic acid, and beta carotene. AFS = 36 drives the score into P3.

The Paradox in Numbers

  • The scatter chart tells the story: as sugar drops from 18 g (Yoplait) to 7 g (Light + Fit), processing climbs from 27 to 51. The trade-off is real.
  • Metabolic class inverts: Fage (whole milk, 5 g sugar) is N0; Yoplait (18 g sugar) is N1a. But Light + Fit (7 g sugar) drops back to N0 — the “healthier” yogurt has a better metabolic score but 10× the processing.
  • AFS dominates the diet end: Light + Fit Banana Cream’s AFS of 36 is higher than many candy bars. Fage’s AFS is 0.
  • MDS drives the mainstream middle: Yoplait’s MDS = 8 (modified starch, corn starch) accounts for most of its score alongside AFS = 9.
