Analysis

Six nut butters and spreads, scores 0 to 34. Natural peanut butter and natural almond butter both score 0 — the nut type doesn’t matter. It’s what you add that counts: sweeteners, hydrogenated oils, emulsifiers, and artificial flavors build the ladder rung by rung.

Key Takeaways

  • Smucker’s Natural (0) and Justin’s Classic Almond (0) both score zero — peanut vs. almond makes no difference when the ingredient list is just nuts and oil.
  • Skippy Natural (4) adds sugar and palm oil. HES = 4 from the added sweetener, but still zero additives. The jump from 0 to 4 is minimal.
  • Justin’s Honey Almond (17) is the surprise: still zero AFS — no chemical additives at all — but honey + organic powdered sugar push HES to 8 and MLS to 7. Clean ingredients can still accumulate processing points through sweetener engineering.
  • Jif Creamy (25) crosses the industrial threshold: fully hydrogenated vegetable oils and mono/diglycerides push MDS to 10 and trigger the first AFS. That one Tier A additive (mono/diglycerides) accounts for all of Jif’s AFS = 6.
  • Nutella (34) — sugar is ingredient #1, not hazelnuts. Vanillin (an artificial flavor) and lecithin give it AFS = 10, but the real driver is MLS = 14: 21 g of sugar per serving.

Sub-score Deep Dive

  • AFS stays at zero for 4 of 6 products. Only Jif (mono/diglycerides) and Nutella (vanillin + lecithin) trigger additive detection. Natural nut butters are inherently additive-free.
  • MDS drives the Jif jump: MDS = 10 from fully hydrogenated vegetable oils is the largest single sub-score leap in the lineup. The oils prevent separation but disrupt the ingredient matrix.
  • HES does more work than expected: Justin’s Honey Almond reaches P1a with zero additives, purely through sweetener layering (honey + powdered sugar = HES 8).
  • The scatter shows uniform fat: 5 of 6 products cluster at 16–19 g fat. Nutella breaks the pattern — lower fat (11 g) but 21 g sugar, the only product where sugar dominates the nutrition profile.
